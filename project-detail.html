Agriculture

Sustainable Farming Practices for Climate Resilience

Duration: 2022 – Ongoing
Funding: Ministry of Agriculture
Principal Investigator: Prof. Sharma
Status: Active

Project Overview

This research project focuses on developing and promoting climate-resilient agricultural practices among small-scale farmers in drought-prone regions of rural India. Through a combination of field experiments, farmer participatory research, and policy analysis, we aim to identify sustainable farming methods that can enhance food security while protecting natural resources.

The project operates across three districts and engages with over 500 farming households. Our approach integrates scientific research with traditional knowledge systems, recognizing the valuable insights that generations of farmers have accumulated about local ecosystems.

Research Objectives

Our research is guided by four primary objectives that address both immediate farmer needs and long-term sustainability goals:

  • Document and evaluate traditional drought-coping agricultural practices
  • Test innovative organic farming techniques in controlled field conditions
  • Develop crop diversification strategies for income stability
  • Create training modules for farmer capacity building
  • Formulate policy recommendations for sustainable agriculture support

Methodology

We employ a mixed-methods research approach combining quantitative surveys, qualitative interviews, and participatory action research. Field trials are conducted in collaboration with local farmers on their own land, ensuring that findings are directly applicable to real-world conditions.

Data collection includes soil health analysis, crop yield measurements, water usage monitoring, and economic assessments. We also conduct focus group discussions and individual interviews to understand farmer perceptions, challenges, and aspirations.

Key Findings

Preliminary results from our research indicate several promising directions for sustainable agriculture in drought-prone areas:

  • Mixed cropping systems show 25% higher resilience to rainfall variability
  • Traditional seed varieties outperform hybrids under water-stress conditions
  • Organic soil amendments improve water retention capacity by 40%
  • Farmer-to-farmer learning networks are more effective than top-down training

Impact & Outreach

Beyond academic research, this project emphasizes translating findings into practical applications. We conduct regular training workshops for farmers, produce educational materials in local languages, and engage with policymakers to advocate for supportive agricultural policies.

Our team has presented findings at national and international conferences, and two research papers have been published in peer-reviewed journals. We also maintain active collaboration with the State Agriculture Department for scaling successful interventions.
