UMA Initiative

Understanding and Mapping Agriculture — A comprehensive framework for rural research and development

What is UMA?

UMA (Understanding and Mapping Agriculture) is a flagship initiative of the Rural Research Group that aims to create a comprehensive knowledge base for understanding agricultural systems, rural livelihoods, and community dynamics across diverse agroecological zones.

Launched in 2020, UMA represents our commitment to bridging the gap between academic research and practical rural development. The initiative combines rigorous scientific methods with participatory approaches, ensuring that the communities we study are also partners in the research process.

Our Vision

To become the leading resource for evidence-based rural development planning and policy formulation in India, empowering communities and policymakers with actionable insights.

Objectives

The UMA initiative is structured around five core objectives that guide our research activities and partnerships:

  • Systematic Documentation: Create detailed profiles of agricultural practices, cropping patterns, and farming systems across different regions
  • Livelihood Mapping: Understand the diverse livelihood strategies of rural households and identify opportunities for improvement
  • Resource Assessment: Evaluate natural resource availability, usage patterns, and sustainability challenges
  • Community Engagement: Build partnerships with village communities for participatory research and knowledge co-creation
  • Policy Interface: Translate research findings into policy recommendations and advocate for evidence-based rural development

Relevance to Rural Research

UMA addresses a critical need in rural development — the lack of comprehensive, ground-level data that accurately captures the complexity of rural life. While government surveys provide macro-level statistics, they often miss the nuances of local practices, challenges, and opportunities.

Through UMA, we create detailed case studies and comparative analyses that inform both academic research and practical interventions. Our data has been used by government departments, NGOs, and development agencies for planning and implementing rural programs.

Key Achievement

UMA has covered over 200 villages across 8 states, documenting diverse agricultural practices and producing 25+ research reports and policy briefs.

Role of the Research Group

The Rural Research Group serves as the institutional anchor for UMA, providing:

  • Academic Leadership: Faculty members guide research design, methodology, and quality assurance
  • Research Training: Graduate students and research associates receive hands-on training in field research methods
  • Data Management: We maintain a secure database of UMA research outputs, accessible to authorized researchers and policymakers
  • Stakeholder Coordination: We facilitate partnerships with government agencies, NGOs, and international organizations
  • Knowledge Dissemination: Through publications, workshops, and conferences, we share UMA findings with diverse audiences

Get Involved

We welcome collaborations with researchers, students, and organizations interested in rural development. Whether you want to contribute to data collection, use UMA data for your research, or partner on specific projects, please reach out to us.
