Unnat Maharashtra Abhiyan

Building an independent and public knowledge infrastructure for Maharashtra's socio-economic and cultural development

What is UMA?

Unnat Maharashtra Abhiyan (UMA) is a project of the Ministry of Higher and Technical Education, Government of Maharashtra. Its mandate is to build an independent and public knowledge infrastructure for the state which will bring socio-economic and cultural development for its people, especially those in the bottom 80% of the socio-economic strata.

UMA aligns closely with the Unnat Bharat Abhiyan (UBA) mechanism of the Ministry of Human Resource Development, Government of India, whose vision is to bring about "transformational change in rural development processes by leveraging knowledge institutions to help build the architecture of an Inclusive India."

Our Vision

To leverage higher education institutions for fostering socio-economic and cultural development across Maharashtra, addressing regional challenges through collaborative research and innovative, community-driven solutions.

Objectives

UBA is being operationalised through UMA, since they both share the same objectives:

  • Curriculum Alignment: To seek alignment of curricula and research with regional development needs, ensuring academic programs address real-world challenges faced by Maharashtra's communities
  • Field-Based Learning: Re-emphasize field-work and case-studies as an important pedagogy, connecting students directly with ground realities of rural development
  • Knowledge Access: Provide a formal mechanism for local bodies such as Zilla Parishads, Gram Panchayats, etc. to access regional engineering colleges, including the IITs, for their knowledge needs
  • Funding & Data: Enable local governance bodies to obtain funding and data support for development initiatives through institutional partnerships
  • Community Engagement: Facilitate collaboration between academic institutions and local communities to identify and address development challenges together

Key Focus Areas

UMA addresses critical aspects of rural and semi-urban development across Maharashtra through demand-driven research and practical interventions:

  • Agricultural Development: Supporting farmers with scientific knowledge, sustainable practices, and technology adoption for improved productivity
  • Water Management: Addressing water scarcity through watershed development, rainwater harvesting, and efficient irrigation systems
  • Livelihood Enhancement: Creating opportunities for income diversification and skill development in rural communities
  • Health & Sanitation: Improving public health outcomes through awareness programs and infrastructure development
  • Education & Capacity Building: Strengthening local institutions and enhancing capabilities of both public and private sectors

Partnership Model

UMA creates a bridge between knowledge institutions and local governance bodies, enabling Gram Panchayats and Zilla Parishads to access research expertise, technical support, and evidence-based solutions for their development challenges.

Role of Our Research Group

The Rural Research Group actively participates in UMA through various activities:

  • Village Adoption: Engaging with adopted villages for comprehensive development planning and implementation support
  • Student Involvement: Deploying students for field research, surveys, and community interaction as part of their academic curriculum
  • Technical Support: Providing technical expertise to local bodies for infrastructure projects, agricultural improvements, and resource management
  • Research Studies: Conducting demand-driven research aligned with regional development priorities
  • Capacity Building: Organizing workshops and training programs for community members and local officials

Get Involved

We welcome students, researchers, and organizations interested in contributing to Maharashtra's rural development through UMA. Whether you want to participate in field activities, contribute to research projects, or partner on specific initiatives, please reach out to us.
